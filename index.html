About Me

I am Md Nayeem Sadman, a deeply motivated international Bachelor's degree holder in Biotechnology from the College of Life Sciences, Guizhou University, a prestigious "double first-class" institution in China. My journey, originating from Bangladesh and into a highly competitive academic environment abroad, has instilled in me a unique global perspective and a deep commitment to harnessing science and technology to improve healthcare and agriculture worldwide. My main goal is to serve as a conduit between pioneering laboratory innovations and their real-world applications. ...... My academic and research interests are focused on the dynamic convergence of agricultural biotechnology and computational biology. I strongly believe in the transformative potential of green biotechnology, especially in the use of cutting-edge technologies such as gene editing to address critical global challenges like food security and environmental sustainability. This conviction drives my research, where I have explored mechanisms controlling plant architecture, such as in the project, “Overexpression of CsBRC, an F-box gene from Camellia sinensis, enhances plant branching in tobacco and rice.” Building directly on this experience, I am now deeply engaged in a targeted gene-editing project focused on a specific F-BOX gene in rice. The successfully edited seeds are currently being cultivated in Hainan for maturation, after which we will conduct detailed phenotypic analysis. Our primary objective is to develop improved rice varieties with enhanced branching, a trait we hypothesize will lead to substantially increased yields and contribute directly to global food security solutions. In parallel, I am fascinated by the data-centric nature of modern biology. I am deeply interested in computational biology and the application of artificial intelligence (AI) to integrate and interpret complex, high-throughput multi-omics (e.g., genomics, proteomics) datasets. I believe that a holistic, systems-level understanding of biological processes can only be achieved through sophisticated analysis of such integrated data. This approach is critical for developing next-generation diagnostics and personalized therapies for complex diseases. Ultimately, my educational pursuits are inextricably linked to my deep commitment to my native Bangladesh. My long-term goal is to apply the knowledge and skills I have acquired to establish state-of-the-art agricultural research centers in Bangladesh. These centers will aim to empower local farmers with stable and high-yielding crop varieties, thereby strengthening the country’s food security and economy. Furthermore, I want to actively contribute to public health initiatives by developing and implementing technology-driven healthcare solutions. Outside the laboratory and lecture hall, I am a passionate advocate for community engagement. My experience as a volunteer with the World Health Organization (WHO) has given me invaluable insight into global public health challenges. My early participation in the Bangladesh Chemistry and Biology Olympiad ignited my passion for scientific inquiry. Today, I channel this passion by mentoring aspiring life science students and young researchers, as I believe that building a collaborative and knowledge-sharing ecosystem is essential for scientific advancement. My guiding principle in all endeavors is an insatiable drive to explore the unknown. #BeCurious
